5 Reasons Pattaya is Southeast Asia's Best Value Golf Destination

By Dean Page 1 March 2026 5 min read

Every Australian golfer I've taken to Pattaya comes back with the same look on their face. It's part disbelief, part delight. They've just played 18 holes on a course that would cost $200 back home, paid about $55 including their caddie, and they're sitting in a clubhouse with a cold Chang beer wondering why they didn't do this years ago.

Pattaya doesn't get the glamour press that Phuket does. It's not as pretty a postcard. But for serious golfers who want great courses at genuine value, it's genuinely hard to beat. Here are five reasons why.

1. The Green Fees Are Extraordinary

Let's start with the number that matters most. In Pattaya, you can play at courses like Laem Chabang International, Phoenix Gold and Burapha for between 1,500 and 2,500 Thai Baht - that's roughly $60 to $100 Australian at current exchange rates. For the Siam Country Club Old Course, one of the finest tracks in Asia, you're looking at around $120 to $150.

Compare that to a comparable-quality course in Australia. Royal Melbourne will set you back $400+ as a visitor. Even a decent private club in Sydney or Brisbane is looking at $150 to $200. In Pattaya, you can play four rounds on genuinely world-class courses for the price of one round at home.

And that green fee almost always includes your caddie. In Australia, a caddie would cost you an extra $100 to $150. In Pattaya, they're part of the package.

2. Course Quality That Punches Way Above Its Price Tag

This is where Pattaya really surprises people. The assumption is that cheap means ordinary. It doesn't. The Siam Country Club Old Course has hosted the LPGA Thailand tournament multiple times. Laem Chabang, designed by Jack Nicklaus, would be considered a landmark course anywhere in the world. Phoenix Gold is immaculately maintained and genuinely fun to play.

Within an hour's drive of Pattaya you have more than 25 courses, ranging from comfortable resort tracks to genuine championship venues. That concentration of quality at that price point doesn't exist anywhere else in Asia. Not Phuket, not Bali, not Vietnam - nowhere.

Our Pattaya golf tour typically takes in four courses over five days, and every single one of them gets a thumbs up from golfers making the trip for the first time.

3. Caddies Are Included and They're Excellent

Walking 18 holes in 30-degree heat carrying your own bag is not what any sensible golfer wants to do. In Pattaya, caddies are mandatory at most courses - which sounds like a burden until you realise how much they improve your round.

Thai caddies know their courses intimately. They'll read greens, club you correctly and carry your bag with a smile for the full 18 holes. Many of them have been doing this for years and know every break and slope on the course. You'll make better decisions, play better golf and enjoy the round more with a good caddie by your side.

The standard tip is around 300 to 500 Baht (roughly $12 to $20 AUD). That's outstanding value for four to five hours of personalised service from someone who genuinely wants to help you play well.

4. Accommodation and Food Are Remarkably Affordable

Value in Pattaya isn't just about the golf. A quality four-star hotel in a central location will cost $60 to $100 a night. A brilliant Thai meal at a local restaurant is $8 to $15. A cold beer at a sports bar is $3. The overall daily cost of being in Pattaya is a fraction of what you'd spend at home or even in Phuket.

For a group of golfers travelling together, this creates an interesting dynamic. You can choose to spend more - there are five-star properties and high-end restaurants available - or you can stretch your budget across more golf days. Most blokes opt for more golf.

5. The Golf Community Is Huge and Welcoming

Pattaya has a larger permanent expat golf community than almost any city in Southeast Asia. That means the infrastructure around golf is mature and well-developed. Transfer services are reliable, starting times are easy to book, courses are well-staffed and the hospitality industry understands what golfers need.

There's also a real camaraderie in Pattaya's golf scene. You'll find Australians, British, Canadians and locals all playing together, and the clubhouse conversations after a round are half the fun. It's a city that's built its identity around golf tourism, and it shows.

If you're weighing up where to go for your next golf trip to Asia, Pattaya deserves serious consideration. The combination of course quality, price and overall experience is hard to argue with. It's not about settling for less - it's about getting more for your dollar than you'd ever expect.
